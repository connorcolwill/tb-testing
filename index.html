Getting Kids Back to Being Kids, Faster

Reducing TB Diagnostic Time from Days to Hours at SickKids

A Quality Improvement Study by Abigail Mathews, MHSc

University of Toronto | The Hospital for Sick Children

↓

The Challenge: When Every Hour Counts

The Situation

When children are suspected of having tuberculosis (TB), they must be isolated in special negative-pressure rooms until test results confirm they're not contagious. At SickKids, this process was taking an average of 5.2 days.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Separation

Children isolated from families for extended periods

⏰

Delayed Care

Medical procedures and treatments postponed

💰

High Costs

~$1,121 per day for specialized isolation rooms

🏥

Limited Resources

Constrained availability of isolation facilities

The Testing Challenge

Traditional TB testing required:

  • Specimens sent to off-site laboratory (Public Health Ontario)
  • Weekend and holiday delays in processing
  • Courier logistics complications
  • Low sensitivity in children (only 6.8% detection rate with traditional smear microscopy)

Old Diagnostic Pathway

Day 1: Specimen Collection
Day 2-3: Transport & Processing
Day 4-5: Results & Decision

Average: 5.2 days in isolation

A Faster Path Forward: GeneXpert Technology

What We Did

Implemented the GeneXpert MTB/RIF Ultra assay—a rapid molecular test that detects TB in under 2 hours, right at the hospital.

The Approach

This quality improvement study used:

  • Workflow mapping and analysis of 900+ specimens
  • Cost-benefit modeling with decision-analytic framework
  • Laboratory validation across pediatric specimen types
  • Stakeholder collaboration across multiple departments

Why This Matters

Investments in rapid diagnostics represent not just institutional efficiency gains, but contributions to broader public health preparedness and equity. As GeneXpert platforms become more widely available following their expanded use during the COVID-19 pandemic, Canadian institutions are well positioned to leverage existing infrastructure to modernize TB diagnostics.

Ultimately, earlier diagnosis and treatment initiation through rapid testing has the potential to improve outcomes across both hospital and community settings—so we can get kids back to being kids faster.
